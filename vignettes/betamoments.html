Moments of the Beta Kernel

Author

Michael Gordy

We seek solutions to the moments and cross-moments of the transformed PIT values when the kernel densities take the form \[ g_\nu(u) = (\alpha_2-\alpha_1)^{1-a-b} (u-\alpha_1)^{a-1} (\alpha_2-u)^{b-1} \mathbb{1}_{\{\alpha_1\leq u\leq\alpha_2\}} \] for parameters \((a>0, b>-1/2)\) and \(0\leq\alpha_1< \alpha_2\leq1\). We draw upon the websites FWC and DLMF, and the books Atlas of Functions (“AF”) by and Table of Integrals, Series, and Products by (“GR”).

Coding is facilitated by computing moments in terms of the moments of standardized beta kernels with \(\alpha_1=0,\alpha_2=1\). Let \(\tilde{\nu}\) denote a beta\((a,b)\) kernel scaled to \([0,1]\) and let \(\tilde{W}=G_{\tilde{\nu}}(U)\) for \(U\sim\text{Uniform}(0,1)\). The uncentered moments of \(W\) can be obtained as: \[\begin{equation} \mathbb{E}[W^k] = \begin{cases} (\alpha_2-\alpha_1)\mathbb{E}[\tilde{W}^k] + (1-\alpha_2)G_{\tilde{\nu}}(1)^k & \text{if } \alpha_2 < 1 \\ (1-\alpha_1)\mathbb{E}[\tilde{W}^k] & \text{if } \alpha_2 = 1 . \end{cases} \end{equation}\] Proposition \(\ref{prop:tailbehavior}\) guarantees that \(\lim_{y\rightarrow1} (1-y)G_{\tilde{\nu}}(y)^k=0\) for \(k=1,2\), so the expression in the \(\alpha_2=1\) case is simply the limit of the expression in the \(\alpha_2<1\) case.

By , the kernel function \(G_{\tilde{\nu}}(u)=B(u; a,b)\) can be expressed in terms of the Gauss hypergeometric function, \({_2}F_1\), in two equivalent forms: \[\begin{align} B(u; a,b) &= \frac{u^a}{a}{_2}F_1(a, 1-b; a+1; u) (\#eq:incbetastandard) \\ &= \frac{u^a(1-u)^b}{a} {_2}F_1(1, a+b; a+1; u). (\#eq:incbetavar) \end{align}\] We will use each of these forms below.

The two forms are equivalent at \(b=0\). Standard algorithms for the incomplete beta function are numerically unstable for \(b\approx 0\) in the neighborhood of \(p=1\). For the interesting special case of \(b=0\) and \(a\in\mathbb{Q}^+\), provides a finite series expansion in elementary functions.

The first moment of \(\tilde{W}\) is \[ \mathbb{E}[\tilde{W}] = \int_0^1 (1-u)g_{\tilde{\nu}}(u)\,du = B(a,1+b) \] Since \(b>-1/2\), this expression presents no difficulties for our application.

Say we have two beta variables with (possibly) different parameters \((a_i,b_i)\) for \(i=1,2\). Let \(\tilde{G}_i\) denote the transform function for kernel \(i\). To get cross-moments and second moments, we need the integral \[\begin{align} \mbox{}& M(a_1,b_1,a_2,b_2) = \int_0^1 (1-u) \tilde{g}_1(u) \tilde{G}_2(u) du \label{eq:defineM} \\ &= \frac{B(a_1+a_2,1+b_1)}{a_2} {_3}F_2(a_2,a_1+a_2,1-b_2; 1+a_2, 1+a_1+a_2+b_1; 1) \label{eq:MoneA} \\ &= \frac{B(a_1+a_2,1+b_1+b_2)}{a_2} {_3}F_2(1,a_1+a_2,a_2+b_2; 1+a_2, 1+a_1+a_2+b_1+b_2; 1) \label{eq:MoneB} \end{align}\] The two forms come from application of GR 7.512.5 to (@ref(eq:incbetastandard)) and (@ref(eq:incbetavar)), respectively. When calculated from its series expansion, equation \(\eqref{eq:MoneA}\) will be numerically stable whenever \(b_2\leq0\) whereas \(\eqref{eq:MoneB}\) will be numerically stable whenever \(b_2>0\).

There are numerous special cases for which \({_3}F_2(1)\) has known closed-form solution.

Case \(b_1=0\)

When \(b_1=0\) and \(b_2\neq0\), we apply FWC 07.27.03.0119.01 to \(\eqref{eq:incbetastandard}\) to obtain \[\begin{equation} M(a_1,0,a_2,b_2) = \frac{1}{a_1}\left(B(a_2, b_2) - B(a_1+a_2, b_2)\right) \end{equation}\] When \(b_1=b_2=0\), we instead apply FWC 07.27.03.0035.01 to \(\eqref{eq:MoneB}\): \[\begin{equation} M(a_1,0,a_2,0) = \frac{1}{a_1}\left(\psi(a_1+a_2)-\psi(a_2)\right) \end{equation}\] where \(\psi\) is the digamma function. One can derive this by noting that FWC 06.14.26.0001.01 and 06.18.06.0004.01 together imply the limit [ _{b} (-B(a,b)) = (a)+ ] where \(\gamma\) is Euler’s gamma.

Case \(b_1=n\in\mathbb{N}\)

Binomial expansion of the term \((1-u)^{b_1}\) in the integral form \(\eqref{eq:defineM}\) of \(M\) leads to \[\begin{equation} M(a_1,n,a_2,b_2)= \sum_{k=0}^n (-1)^k\binom{n}{k} M(a_1+k,0,a_2,b_2) \end{equation}\] This approach would be practical for small values of \(n\).

Case \(a_2=n\in\mathbb{N}\)

For the moment, assume \(b_2\neq0\). We have from FWC 06.19.03.0003.01: \[\begin{equation} B(u; n, b) = B(n,b)\left( 1-(1-u)^b\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(b)_k u^k}{k!}\right) \end{equation}\] where \((b)_k\) is Pochhammer’s notation defined recursively by \((b)_0=1\), \((b)_{m+1}=(b+m)\cdot (b)_m\). This leads to \[\begin{equation} \label{eq:betaaaN} M(a_1,b_1,n,b_2) = \frac{(n-1)!}{(b_2)_n}\left(B(a_1,1+b_1) - \sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(b_2)_k}{k!} B(a_1+k,1+b_1+b_2)\right). \end{equation}\] This can be computed more effectively in recursive form: \[\begin{align} M(a_1,b_1,1,b_2) &= \frac{1}{b_2}\left(B(a_1, 1+b_1) - B(a_1, 1+b_1+b_2)\right) \label{eq:Mrecursestop} \\ M(a_1,b_1,m+1,b_2) &= \frac{1}{b_2+m}\left(m\; M(a_1,b_1,m,b_2)-B(a_1+m, 1+b_1+b_2)\right) \label{eq:Mrecursenext} \end{align}\]

To take the limit of \(\eqref{eq:Mrecursestop}\) as \(b_2\rightarrow0\), we have by FWC 06.18.06.0013.01 that [ B(a,b+z) = B(a,b)(1+ z((b+1)-(a+b+1)) + (z^2)) ] which implies \[\begin{equation} \label{eq:Monerecursestopzero} M(a_1,b_1,1,0) = B(a_1,1+b_1)\left(\psi(1+a_1+b_1) - \psi(1+b_1)\right) \end{equation}\] Observe that \(\eqref{eq:Mrecursenext}\) is well-behaved at \(b_2=0\).

We have from FWC 06.19.03.0001.01: \[\begin{equation} \label{eq:Bxam} B(u;a, 1+m) = B(a,1+m)\,u^a\sum_{k=0}^m\frac{(a)_k (1-u)^k}{k!} \end{equation}\] Substituting into the integral for \(M\): \[\begin{equation} M(a_1,b_1,a_2,n) = \frac{(n-1)!}{(a_2)_n} \sum_{k=0}^{n-1}\frac{(a_2)_k}{k!} B(a_1+a_2,b_1+k+1). \end{equation}\] This could also be expressed compactly in recursive form. An especially simple subcase is \[\begin{equation} \label{eq:Mbetatwoisone} M(a_1,b_1,a_2,1) = \frac{1}{a_2} B(a_1+a_2,b_1+1). \end{equation}\]

PIT values above \(\alpha_2\) contribute to \(\mathbb{E}[W]\) in the term \((1-\alpha_2)G_\nu(\alpha_2)\).
